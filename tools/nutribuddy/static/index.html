A/B Stock Split ?
Why A/B?

Ca²⁺ + SO₄²⁻ → CaSO₄ precipitation. By separating Part A (Ca) from Part B (SO₄, PO₄), high concentrations stay stable.

Never mix dry A + B together.

BILL OF MATERIALS — SUPPLIER ORDER ?
Stock Concentration

All volumes assume a 100× concentrate. The final mixing step dilutes 1 part concentrate with 99 parts water.

For 50× or 200× concentrates, multiply/divide all volumes proportionally. Higher concentration = smaller volumes but less precision. Lower concentration = larger volumes but easier to measure accurately.
